AllTact Fin Ray: A Compliant Robot Gripper with Omni-Directional Tactile Sensing

Siwei Liang, Yixuan Guan, Jing Xu, Hongyu Qian, Xiangjun Zhang, Dan Wu, Wenbo Ding, Rui Chen,
Tsinghua University
TASE2025

Abstract

Tactile sensing plays a crucial role in robot grasping and manipulation by providing essential contact information between the robot and the environment. In this paper, we present AllTact Fin Ray, a novel compliant gripper design with omni- directional and local tactile sensing capabilities. The finger body is unibody-casted using transparent elastic silicone, and a camera positioned at the base of the finger captures the deformation of the whole body and the contact face. Due to the global deformation of the adaptive structure, existing vision-based tactile sensing approaches that assume constant illumination are no longer applicable. To address this, we propose a novel sensing method where the global deformation is first reconstructed from the image using edge features and spatial constraints. Then, detailed contact geometry is computed from the brightness differ- ence against a dynamically retrieved reference image. Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness of our proposed gripper design and sensing method in contact detection, force estimation, object grasping, and precise manipulation.




Hardware Design

Click here to view the detailed production process

The whole finger body is cast in transparent elastic silicone such that it can deform and fit to the object during grasping and manipulation. We change the original design of the Fin Ray finger and thicken the contact-side in order to capture the contact face using the camera at the bottom of the finger. An extra layer of semitransparent silicone is cast above the contact face for local geometry reconstruction. The surfaces of the finger body are further painted black to block the environmental illumination.

The sensor utilizes simple white LEDs for illumination. Additionally, we incorporate an isolating ring between the camera and LEDs to prevent direct light from the LEDs from entering the camera, ensuring accurate imaging. The side faces feature dotted markers along the edges, which are tracked during sensing to obtain the reference image corresponding to real-time image and accomplish reconstruction. Due to the perspective projection of the imaging process, the radius and spacing of the markers increase proportionally with their distance from the camera, ensuring relatively uniform marker sizes in the captured image.

An AllTact Fin Ray Finger is fabricated through a multi-step process: First, the finger base, sensor shell, and casting molds are 3D-printed using white and black resins. The finger body is unibody-cast with transparent silicone for structural rigidity, followed by a 1.5 mm soft semi-transparent contact layer, and a thin, dyed black, non-transparent layer for tactile sensitivity. After solidifying, the finger is demolded, then the finger is assembled by adhering it to the base, securing the sensor shell with screws, integrating an LED board with an isolating ring, and mounting a 1080p camera (85° FOV). Finally, the surface is coated with black paint to reduce ambient light interference, and laser-engraved dot markers are added for geometry reconstruction. The direct casting of layers ensures robust bonding during deformation.
